Summary:The grievor observed Orthodox religious holidays and had requested vacation leave to observe them shortly before the designated holidays for Western Good Friday and Easter Monday - the employer granted the vacation leave request - shortly after the designated holidays for Good Friday and Easter Monday had passed, the grievor requested paid leave instead - the employer denied the grievor’s request for paid leave - the grievor alleged adverse discrimination because employees who observe Western Good Friday and Easter Monday receive days off with pay, while he is required to use earned vacation days - in dismissing the grievance, the adjudicator stated that the sole issue before him was whether the employer met its obligation to accommodate - the adjudicator found that the accommodation offered by the employer satisfied tests in the jurisprudence - accommodation does not need to be perfect - the collective agreement provided reasonable options to allow the grievor to fulfill his religious obligations, including options other than using vacation leave - at the hearing, the grievor alleged that he should have been allowed to work Western Good Friday and Easter Monday, to avoid taking vacation time to observe Orthodox holidays - the adjudicator found that there was no evidence that the grievor’s solution was ever raised before his first request for vacation leave - the adjudicator noted that if the grievor does not tell the employer that he needs or wants an accommodation, a solution cannot be implemented by the employer, since it does not know that accommodation is being requested - the grievor did not meet his obligation in the accommodation process because he did not make his request known to the employer at a point at which the employer could respond to it. Grievance dismissed.
