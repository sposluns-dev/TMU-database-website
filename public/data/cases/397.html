These claims were joined because the claimants are mother and daughter. Submissions received and considered.2 Allegations [2] The following is a summary of the claimants' several allegations which can be found in their Personal Information Forms narratives. The essence of the claim against Israel is they had many problems in Israel because they are Christian and of ethnic Russian background. They had problems in Armenia because her husband's mother did not want to meet the claimant or her daughter because they are considered Jewish. The claimant's father-in-law liked the claimant's daughter, XXXXX, and decided to take her away from her. They were left without financial support and were smuggled into Israel. XXXXX mother was accepted as the widow of a Jew and they were offered to rent an apartment in XXXXX, XXXXX and the lease proved to be very expensive. XXXXX daughter was harassed in school because she didn't speak Hebrew, because of lack of funding for Hebrew lessons and was mercilessly beaten by the children at school. The claimant went to the police who stated they could not help her. Her daughter's cross was torn off in school and she was beaten. Nobody was punished for that. [3] The claimant found work washing floors and toilets in the gym. The manager, XXXXX, made passes to her, which the claimant ignored. After three months, he found the claimant alone and tried to rape her. She escaped but did not go to work the next day and was fired. She went to the police in XXXXX XXXXX, who called her Russian prostitute and advised her to behave with modesty or she might get arrested as a prostitute. XXXXX found out about the complaint to the police and threatened to turn her daughter into a prostitute and drug addict, if she did not shut up and banish. He told her that he had nothing to fear, as he was a respected, wounded and decorated war veteran, and she was nobody. [4] XXXXX suffered a heart attack in early 2005 and went to the hospital. They wanted to release her, while she was still very weak, but XXXXX insisted on more transfusions until she got a bit stronger. In XXXXX 2006, her mother was placed in a rehabilitation centre. XXXXX and her daughter brought her and left there some really nice clothes. The next day, the clothing was all gone; she was crying, wet with porridge smeared on her face. A Russian-speaking nurse there stated that an orderly got furious when she saw the cross and tore it off. The nurse returned the cross to XXXXX and advised her to take XXXXX home, if they wanted her to live. [5] The claimants moved to XXXXX and the Lebanon-Israel conflict started. XXXXX was getting 250 to 300 rockets a day and XXXXX had a nervous breakdown. The claimants came to Canada. Decision [6] I find that the claimants are not Convention refugees and not persons in need of protection, as they do not have a fear of persecution for a Convention ground in Israel. I also find that the claimants re not persons in need of protection in that their removal to Israel would not subject them personally to a risk to their lives or grounds to believe that their removal to Israel will subject them personally to a danger of torture. Analysis [7] In reaching a decision, I have taken into account the Chairperson's Guidelines to Women Seeking Refugee Protection Based on their Gender.3 This claim turns on credibility, the claimant's unwillingness to proceed in a diligent manner with her claim and state protection. XXXXX was unable to attend the hearings because she was diagnosed with myriad health matters and Alzheimer's.4 The designated representative was present at the hearings in her stead. XXXXX was also provided with a designated representative who appeared at later hearings to assist her. XXXXX did the testifying on behalf of herself and her mother. Her daughter, XXXXX, was disjoined from the claim due to her apprehension by the Catholic Children's Aid upon their request. [8] The claimant complained to the FF.C.T. Refuge Centre that her counsel, Mr. XXXXX, did not send in the appropriate narrative and would not assist her in preparation for her hearing, in a letter from the above submitted to the Board. She repeated these comments at the first sitting when she refused to sign the amended narrative he sent in.5 The claimant was instructed to make a complaint to the Bar Association so counsel was provided with the ability to defend himself against complaints which the claimant had made public by addressing them to the F.C.T. Refugee Center. The claimant did not do this and subsequent counsel stated that counsel XXXXX was a member in good standing with the Bar and a competent counsel. This, however, did not give Mr. XXXXX the right to defend himself with respect to the F.C.J. Refugee Center's records. On a balance of probabilities, I find the claimant failed to carry out instructions of the Board and was willing to impugn the reputation of a competent counsel. In addition, the first PIF was filled out by counsel, XXXXX XXXXX, and had the essence of the claimant's narrative, which was added to and revised several times. Therefore, on a balance of probabilities, I find the claimant not to be a credible or a trustworthy witness. [9] The history of the claimant's request record for postponements is extensive. The following is a record of the requests for postponement: * October 17, 2008 - the first hearing date was postponed on application by counsel and claimant, counsel of record being Mr. XXXXX; * March 3, 2009 - hearing postponed due to changing counsel to Ms. XXXXX; * April 30, 2009 - hearing adjourned due to lack of time, some of which was because of the vagueness and unresponsiveness of the claimant's testimony; * August 12, 2009 - postponement requested and granted made by counsel and claimant; * November 4, 2009 - postponement requested by counsel in a letter of October 20, 2009 because the claimant was going through a very stressful time. This request was granted; however, the claimant appeared, and was unable to proceed because counsel was absent; * February 17, 2010 - Mr. XXXXX requested a postponement due to being previously booked for an ID matter. This request was denied because the onus is on the claimant to retain a counsel who is available to attend her hearing. The decision was made that there are no exceptional circumstances for allowing the application. The claimant has had adequate time to prepare and the Board has considered previous delays and the reason for the delay and that counsel is experienced. The original PIF was signed October 20, 2006. The latest request for a postponement which was denied was made on February 17, 2010. Counsel did appear on the day of the hearing. [10] The claimant has a history of trying to delay her hearing. On a balance of probabilities, I find the claimant not to be diligent in pursuing her claim. If the claimant truly fears persecution in her own country, on a balance of probabilities, I find she would pursue her claim with due diligence. Therefore, on a balance of probabilities, I find the claimant not to be a credible or trustworthy witness. [11] The claimant told her new counsel, Mr. XXXXX, that she did not know why a designated representative was needed. A letter to the Board from her previous counsel, Ms. XXXXX, dated August 8, 2009, stated that she had serious misgivings about the claimant being a designated representative for her daughter. In that same letter, she told the Board that her claimant was incoherent and suicidal. Therefore, Ms. XXXXX was appointed the designated representative for XXXXX. The designated representative's role is to help the claimant make decisions and make sure the wishes of the claimant are carried out and advise the claimant on how to do so. The claimant had been advised by her previous counsel about the need, agreed to by the Member for a designated representative, and knew or ought to have known the reason for being provided with her designated representative. While counsel XXXXX stated that XXXXX problems were under control with medication, this does not detract from XXXXX knowledge about the reason for the necessity of a designated representative. On a balance of probabilities, I find the claimant not to be a credible or trustworthy witness. [12] With respect to the merits of the claim, the outcome is based on credibility and state protection. The claimant bases her personal fear on the attempted rape by Mr. XXXXX and to the lack of police protection. She also based her fear on the treatment of her daughter in school and the lack of either police or school authorities to protect her. Even though her daughter's claim has been disjoined, on a balance of probabilities, I find that the treatment of her daughter would be one of major concern to the claimant. [13] The claimant stated that when she went to the police to complain, they made racist remarks in their refusal to assist her and called her a Russian prostitute. The claimant was asked if she made any further efforts to obtain state protection, to which she replied that she did not. The Federal Court has held that is not sufficient to simply state that police protection was denied in a Democratic state and that claimants must make further attempts to obtain state protection when it is available in a democracy.6 [14] Because I have found the claimant not to be a credible or trustworthy witness, I prefer the evidence and the documentary evidence because it is gathered by a number of human rights and other organizations with no interest in this or any refugee claim. [15] Israel is a parliamentary democracy, with the separation of powers between the Knesset, executive and Judiciary.7 There is a right to a fair trial, and the independent judiciary has enforced this right. The criminal justice system is adversarial and professional judges decide these cases. An independent and impartial judiciary adjudicates lawsuits for damages and for the cessation of human rights offenses.8 Criminal administrative tools focus on the prevention of racially motivated crimes in Israel. Racism is defined in the Penal Law as constituting persecution, humiliation, degradation, a display of enmity; hostility and violence, or causing violence based on race, color, religion or national affiliation. The broadness of this legislation has allowed for a wide application by the Attorney General.9 [16] Police corruption is not a problem in Israel.10 The police are in training programs to promote human rights and cultural sensitivity.11 The police utilize training programs and complaints were reviewed and indictments are recommended for officers.12 The claimant added that she did not make further complaints because the state had failed to protect her. On a balance of probabilities, I find the claimant did not avail herself of the programs that the state has provided in order to ensure that police respond to complaints and, if they do not, are disciplined. On a balance of probabilities, I find the claimant did not rebut the presumption that states can protect their citizens. [17] The claimant was asked if she complained to any of the state-provided organizations that provide women with protection in cases of sexual harassment or domestic abuse. The claimant stated that they were not available in the small city in which she lived and, in addition, she did not know about them. The documents show that Israel has enacted laws to assist women: * Equal Rights for Women Law * Penal laws * Prevention of Family Violence Law * Sexual Harassment Law/Prevention of the Stalking Law which has resulted in an increase in the number of restraining orders. * Victims Offense Rights Law * Limitation on Return of the Sexual Offender to the victim of the Offense's Surrounding.13 [18] Therefore, on a balance of probabilities, I find that the state of Israel has enacted laws with the express purpose of protecting women from domestic violence and workplace sexual harassment. [19] The following resources are available to women who were victims of sexual harassment in the workplace. The Ministry off Industry, Trade and Labor, where women can complain to the division for the employment status of women. Legal aid can be obtained from the Ministry of Justice. Private-sector workers can find assistance in the Operation Harassment at the Ministry of Industry, the Trade and Labor where inspectors will visit the workplace and encourage dialogue. Tribunals deal with harassment in the private sector. Reducing inequality and ensuring respect for civil rights for citizens belonging to minority sectors has been a central priority. Ensuring equal integration of non-Jewish citizens in the civilian society and ensuring equality and access to education, health care and welfare.14 That Ombudsman's office lodges complaints against government offices and is open for anyone or group feeling an injustice.15 The claimant was asked if she went to any of these groups to complain, with respect to the attempted rape. She stated that she did not because she did not know anything about them. The claimant is a well educated woman,16 who, although she has some psychological problems, has the ability to obtain information from the state of the Israel as to what options are available to her. The claimant merely went to the police who refused to help her. On a balance of probabilities, I find the claimant did not access any of the myriad options open to her to ensure her protection in the state of Israel. Therefore, on a balance of probabilities, I find the claimant has not rebutted the presumption that states can protect their citizens. [20] With respect to her concerns about mistreatment of her daughter, on a balance of probabilities, given the above avenues to obtain state protection, I find the claimant has not rebutted the presumption that states can protect their citizens. [21] With respect to religious identity and ethnicity, from 2007 onwards, the Ministry of Interior no longer required that identity cards carry nationality. Christians are approximately 2% of the population and non-Jews around 24% of the population.17 There is freedom of religion, although there is tension among all religious groups to some extent.18 Both recognized and unrecognized religious communities generally practice religions freely. There are no reports on societal abuses or discrimination based on religious belief or practice.19 Messianic and evangelical groups have grown in Israel, despite some harassment.20 The claimant belongs to neither an evangelical nor a messianic group in Israel. In addition, the harassment based on religion maybe reported to the ombudsman. Thus, it is open to both claimants to report the religious harassment to the ombudsman. Therefore, on a balance of probabilities, since the claimant did not complain to the ombudsman, I find that she failed to rebut the presumption that states can protect their citizens. [22] The claimant stated that she could not live in a Russian population district in Israel because they were considered to be non-Jews and will be subject to persecution. The Russian population and Israel has its own identity, with a distinct economic, social and political infrastructure. There are Russian schools, stores and neighbourhoods.21 There are over one million Russians in Israel and about 300,000 of these are considered non-Jewish. People can and do get by speaking only Russian.22 Concerns over Russian children integrated into school programs have led to initiatives to promote their educational and social integration.23 Freedom of movement is provided for, under the law in Israel.24 Therefore, on a balance of probabilities, I find that the claimants can relocate to any Russian neighbourhood and they can seek redress for any societal discrimination.25 [23] I have found the claimant not to be credible and not to have rebutted the presumption that states can protect their citizens and that they can relocate to a predominantly Russian neighbourhood, where services are provided in Russian. Therefore, I find that there is not a serious possibility or reasonable chance that the claimants would face persecution for a Convention ground, if they return to Israel. Therefore, the claimants are not Convention refugees. [24] The claimant, XXXXX, tendered a psychological evaluation by Dr. XXXXX, who found that the claimant was experiencing a pervasive sense of victimization, that she is suffering from a major depressive episode, severe, as well as post-traumatic stress disorder.26 This diagnosis was based on an interview with the claimant, who presented the same allegations as those in her PIF. While I accept the doctor's diagnosis, with respect to the above mental conditions, because I have found the claimant not to be credible and not to have rebutted the presumption that states can protect their citizens, I give this no weight with respect to the claim. The claimant, XXXXX, has been diagnosed with advanced dementia; however, I have accepted this diagnosis, have appointed a designated representative for her and she was not required to appear at the hearing due to her disability.27 However, I give this diagnosis no weight with respect to this claim [25] The panel then turned its mind to whether the claimants would be subject personally to a risk to their lives, or to cruel and unusual treatment or punishment, or whether there are substantial grounds to believe that they would be subject personally to a danger of torture, if they return to Israel. Based on the above analysis and the claimant's lack of credibility, I find, on a balance of probabilities, that there are no such risks. Conclusion [26] Having considered all of the evidence and based on the above analysis, I find the claimants not to be Convention refugees and not to be persons in need of protection, and I reject their claims.
