Cochrane Place and Date of Hearing: Vancouver, British Columbia June 1, 2021 Place and Date of Judgment: Vancouver, British Columbia June 14, 2021 Summary: Court granted order that security for costs of trial and for anticipated costs in this court be paid within 30 days and that the appeal be stayed in the meantime. Appeal is devoid of merit; consequently, a no-fee order was refused despite appellant's impecuniosity. Reasons for Judgment of the Honourable Madam Justice Newbury: [1] There are before me two applications. The first is brought by Mr. Beaumont for a 'no-fee' order in respect of his appeal of an order of Mr. Justice Branch in the Supreme Court of British Columbia dated January 21, 2021 striking this action in its entirety. The second application is brought by the defendants for an order that Mr. Beaumont deposit or pay security for costs in the amount of $16,048.36 and that the appeal be stayed pending payment into court, with leave to apply to dismiss the appeal after 30 days if security is not posted. As I understand it $16,048.36 is the total of the estimated costs of the appeal, being $4,569.60, plus the defendants' estimated trial costs (not yet assessed), with disbursements, of $11,478.76. [2] There were several irregularities in the court below, and judging by the grounds of appeal Mr. Beaumont has set out in his notice of application for leave to appeal, the irregularities continue. I will not set out in full his grounds of appeal, but the following extract is representative: "the Honourable [chambers judge] had no knowledge whatsoever of this case and did not read properly the file, and from the beginning started to tell me that I should have a proper lawyer, which is not his mission. He was very bias [sic] and was very incompetent in this case as he never dealt with such similar, as he was appointed for three years only as judge in the Supreme Court he kept and [spent] the majority of his time going back [and] forth consulting the Code Civil, in another word he was completely lost and the Counsel Mme. Anne Cochrane detected that he has a huge deficiency and kept presenting different cases which I wasn't aware of Mme. Anne Cochrane then took advantage of a weak and incompetent judge. And manipulated him at ease. When it came time to present my side, here is what I said to the Honourable [chambers judge], "your Honour this is not a Banana Republic" and left without presenting my side, because my deepest intuition and HIS attitude was very obvious where he was leading [3] Ms. Cochrane has provided affidavit evidence of her assistant, confirming that the bill of costs for the Supreme Court hearing was sent to Mr. Beaumont on May 5, 2020. In response, he emailed to the assistant the following message: You are not going to have any DIME, Furthermore, I am going to file a lawsuit implicating you in a HUGE conspiracy as it was you who confirmed the name of Christis and not Chrissie. I will also prepare all of the documents related to your attitude during all of these proceedings and send them to the Vancouver Bar in order to disbar you Regarding the Decision of [the chambers judge], this case is not closed yet. [4] With respect to the 'no-fee' order, Mr. Beaumont is aged 72 and has filed evidence that indicates he has monthly income of $1,316.85 and monthly expenses of $1,450. He also claims to be indebted to someone in the amount of $37,500. He deposes that he has a Ph.D. in political science and other graduate degrees and that he worked until 2011 as a "senior sales national manager" of something called International Profit Association or "IPA". [5] I am satisfied that if this information is correct, Mr. Beaumont is impecunious. It is also clear he has no intention of paying any costs order in either court and that should his appeal fail, the defendants as the successful parties would be unable to collect costs. [6] I am also satisfied that given the grounds of appeal I have described, Mr. Beaumont's appeal is devoid of merit. [7] In the result, I am dismissing Mr. Beaumont's application for a no-fee order and granting the order for security for costs in the amount of $16,048.36, to be deposited with the Registrar no later than 30 days from the date of this order. The appeal will be stayed in the meantime. If the security is not posted within 30 days, the defendants or any of them will be at liberty to apply to have the appeal dismissed. [8] The defendants shall have their costs of this application and given the irregularities experienced thus far between the parties, I will order that the appellant's signature on this order be dispensed with. "The Honourable Madam Justice Newbury"
